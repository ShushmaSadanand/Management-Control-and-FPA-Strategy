## Project: Corporate Management Control & FP&A in the Luxury Industry (Chanel)
**Files:** `G09_Team10_Management control.pdf` (Comprehensive Field Report) & `Chanel_Management_Control.pdf` (Executive Presentation)

A rigorous field-research project and strategic evaluation of management control systems inside **Chanel**, constructed around an exclusive executive interview with the *International Management Controller (FP&A) of Chanel's Watches & Fine Jewellery division*.

### Core Business & Operational Insights
* **FP&A in High-Premium Markets:** Analyzed how a premier, privately-held luxury house balances historical brand exclusivity, multi-division revenue growth, and strict regional control across decentralized global territories (Europe, North America, and the Swiss Market).
* **Agile Budgeting & Crisis Management:** Evaluated Chanel's operational resilience during major market shocks (such as pandemic travel limitations), highlighting the tactical transition to quarterly revised rolling forecasts, aggressive travel overhead reductions, and localized resource shifts to stable consumer zones.
* **Innovative Controls & Workflow Automation:** Documented forward-looking corporate governance practices, specifically Chanel's development and deployment of customized internal, secure GPT-based chatbot environments designed to streamline analyst data discovery without risking highly confidential corporate financials.
* **The Controller as a Strategic Business Partner:** Outlined modern accountability strategies where controllers pivot from strict policy "enforcers" to cross-functional "support systems"—relying on combined qualitative insights and verified multi-system data streams to empower local team autonomy and calculated, trend-driven risk-taking.

### Key Competencies Demonstrated
* Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) & Rolling Forecast Frameworks
* Management Control Systems (MCS) & Organizational Performance Measurement
* Luxury Brand Operations, Corporate Governance, & Strategic Resource Allocation
